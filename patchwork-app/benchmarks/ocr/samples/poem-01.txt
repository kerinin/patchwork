THE MORNING TRAIN

	The platform empty, still, and grey,
	Before the crowd arrives --
	I stand alone and watch the rails
	Stretch out like silver knives.

	A distant whistle, low and long,
	Cuts through the morning air.
	The engine rounds the final bend,
	All steam and smoke and glare.

	The doors slide open, people rush,
	A hundred lives collide.
	And I step on, and find my seat,
	And watch the world outside.

		* * *

	The city shrinks behind the glass,
	The fields roll into view.
	And somewhere in this rocking car,
	I start my life anew.

				-- Anonymous, 1947
