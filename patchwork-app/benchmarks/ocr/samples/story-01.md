THE CLOCK ON THE MANTLE

	The house had been empty for eleven years when
Sarah finally returned. She stood in the doorway,
her suitcase at her feet, and listened.

	Silence. Complete and heavy.

	No -- not quite. From somewhere deeper in the
house came a sound. Tick. Tick. Tick. The old
grandfather clock in the parlor, still keeping time
after all these years. Who had been winding it?

	She stepped inside. The floorboards creaked
beneath her feet, announcing her presence to no one
in particular. Dust covered everything -- the tables,
the chairs, the photographs on the walls. Faces she
barely recognized anymore.

	The parlor door was closed.

	"Hello?" she called out, feeling foolish.

	Tick. Tick. Tick.
